Visit Kanab, UT!

"Little Hollywood" of the West

Kanab earned the nickname "Little Hollywood" because it was a major filming location for classic Western movies and TV shows. Over 100 films and series were shot here, including The Lone Ranger, Gunsmoke, and Westworld. You can even visit the Little Hollywood Movie Museum to see old movie sets!

Gateway to National Parks

Kanab is perfectly located near some of the most famous national parks in the U.S.:

  • Zion National Park (40 minutes away)
  • Bryce Canyon National Park (1.5 hours away)
  • Grand Canyon (North Rim) (1.5 hours away)
  • Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument is right next door!

Coral Pink Sand Dunes

The Coral Pink Sand Dunes State Park is a must-visit! These bright pink and orange dunes are perfect for sandboarding, ATV riding, and photography. The dunes are constantly shifting, making every visit unique.

Star Wars & Ancient Petroglyphs

Kanab’s desert landscapes have been used in Star Wars: The Mandalorian and other sci-fi projects. Plus, you can see ancient petroglyphs from the Ancestral Puebloans at places like Paria Canyon.

Buckskin Gulch – The Longest Slot Canyon in the World

This epic slot canyon near Kanab stretches for over 21 miles and is considered the longest and deepest slot canyon in the world. It’s an adventurer’s dream with towering walls and breathtaking formations.

The Wave

One of the most stunning and exclusive hikes in the world, The Wave, is just outside Kanab in Arizona’s Vermilion Cliffs National Monument. The hike requires a permit, which is hard to get, but the surreal, swirling sandstone formations make it a bucket-list experience.
