Desertification: The Drying of the Planet

Extent of Drying Lands

Over the past 30 years, approximately 77.6% of Earth's land areas have become drier due to human-induced climate change. This trend has led to the expansion of drylands by about 4.3 million square kilometers globally.

Impact on Agriculture

Desertification severely affects agricultural productivity. In regions like sub-Saharan Africa, significant reductions in crop yields are anticipated, exacerbating food insecurity and economic challenges.

Health Consequences

Aridification leads to health issues such as increased childhood stunting and reduced water supplies, particularly in vulnerable regions.

  • higher threats of malnutrition from reduced food and water supplies
  • more water- and food-borne diseases that result from poor hygiene and a lack of clean water
  • respiratory diseases caused by atmospheric dust from wind erosion and other air pollutants
  • the spread of infectious diseases as populations migrate.

Global Initiatives

In December 2024, the Climate Investment Funds (CIF) approved a $500 million initiative to aid Ethiopia in restoring degraded lands, protecting forests, and enhancing food security. This program aims to restore over 320,000 hectares across various regions.

Sustainable Agriculture

Adopting drought-resistant crops and efficient irrigation methods are essential strategies to mitigate the impacts of desertification on agriculture.
